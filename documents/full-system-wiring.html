Full System Wiring

ESP32-C3 controller → PCF8575 I/O expander → PCA9685 LED driver → LEDs. Two power rails, one shared ground — and the 5 V never touches the PCF8575.

The whole design in one sentence: everything logic lives on a single 3.3 V rail (safe for the C3's GPIOs), the LEDs live on a separate 5 V rail, and all grounds are tied together. The PCF8575 board runs fully at 3.3 V (its VCC–VDD bridge closed), so it never sees 5 V.

Wiring Diagram

Power flows up/down to the two horizontal rails; the blue I²C bus runs along the bottom with a single pair of pull-ups to 3.3 V. Both the PCF8575 and the PCA9685 hang off that same bus (different addresses). The 5 V rail feeds only the PCA9685's V+ and the LEDs — it stops short of the PCF8575.

Why it's split this way

  • 3.3 V logic island. The C3's GPIOs aren't 5 V-tolerant. Keeping the PCF8575 (bridge closed) and both I²C devices on one 3.3 V rail means SDA/SCL idle at 3.3 V — no level shifting, no risk.
  • 5 V only where it earns its keep. The USB-C wall plug's current headroom is for the LEDs. That 5 V goes to the PCA9685's V+ (the LED rail) and nowhere near the expander.
  • One common ground. The two rails differ in voltage but must share a single ground reference, or the PWM signals have nothing to switch against.
  • PCA9685 has two supplies on purpose. VCC = logic (3.3 V, matches I²C), V+ = LED power (5 V). They're separate pins — don't tie them together.
Never bridge the two worlds on a PCF8575 pin. The expander sinks current but barely sources any, and its pins top out at its 3.3 V rail. If you drive an indicator straight off a P-pin, its high side goes to the port VDD (3.3 V), never the 5 V rail. All 5 V LED current stays on the PCA9685's V+. Per-LED resistor values and the sink-wiring detail are in pcf8575-led-wiring.html.

Firmware note: initialise I²C with Wire.begin(4, 5) — GPIO4/5 avoid the C3's strapping pins (GPIO2/8/9), and GPIO8/9 are already used for the status LED and BOOT button.
